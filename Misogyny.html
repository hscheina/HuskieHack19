Misogyny

Misogyny and Sexism within STEM starts at a young age. Children are taught at a young age to associate stereotypes with specific genders. Girls like pink and play with Barbies and princesses. Boys like blue and play with trucks and action figures. We are constantly taught, even through subliminal messages, that men are more significant than women. These stereotypes continue into adulthood and into the workplace.

Almost half of the amount of women in STEM experience gender discrimination at work. That's compared to 19% of men that have experiencd at a STEM workplace. As women, we need to work together in order to combat this issue and build a support system against mysogyny.
