Misogyny and Sexism within STEM starts at a young age. Children are taught at a young age to associate stereotypes with specific genders. Girls like pink and play with Barbies and princesses. Boys like blue and play with trucks and action figures. We are constantly taught, even through subliminal messages, that men are more significant than women. These stereotypes continue into adulthood and into the workplace. Almost half of the amount of women in STEM experience gender discrimination at work. That's compared to 19% of men that have experiencd at a STEM workplace. As women, we need to work together in order to combat this issue and build a support system against mysogyny. The Mom Project - The Mom Project is a startup, aimed towards helping moms return to the workforce with positions they hold the qualifications for. https://themomproject.com/ Brotopia: Breaking up the Boy's Club of Silicon Valley by Emily Chang - This book uncovers the true secrets of Silicon Valley, how the industry seems to favor men over women, and how we have started working to overcome this. http://www.brotopiabook.com/ National Girls Collaborative Project: This organization works towards informing and empowering girls in STEM. They provide funding for clubs, activities, and research grants. https://ngcproject.org/about-ngcp
